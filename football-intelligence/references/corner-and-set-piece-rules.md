# Corner and Set-Piece Rules

**Purpose:** Fix the model's weakest market by replacing generic corner averages with mechanism-based corner analysis.

This file formalizes the Jordan-Algeria lesson and expands it into reusable pre-match and live rules.

---

## Why corners were failing

The model previously treated corners as a side effect of possession, dominance, or expected scoreline. That is wrong.

Corners are produced by mechanisms:

- Wide attacks and blocked crosses.
- Defensive clearance behavior.
- Underdog exit quality.
- Set-piece taker quality.
- Score state.
- Whether the favorite is chasing or managing.
- Whether the underdog protects the box or presses before crosses happen.

A team can dominate and produce few corners if it attacks centrally or scores quickly. A narrow 2-1 match can produce huge corners if the trailing team attacks through wide/set-piece pressure.

---

## Corner mechanism checklist

Before any corner prediction, answer:

1. Which team will have more territory?
2. Does that team attack wide, centrally, or directly?
3. Does the opponent block crosses or stop attacks before the cross?
4. Does the favorite have elite set-piece delivery?
5. Does either team have strong aerial targets?
6. Can the underdog escape pressure with sustained counters?
7. What happens to corners if the underdog scores first?
8. What happens to corners if the favorite scores first?
9. Are corners likely to be generated by siege, transition, or set-piece routines?
10. Is the corner pick still valid across at least two scenario branches?

If questions 2, 3, 4, 6, and 7 are unknown, do not make a Tier 1 corner pick.

## Rule #39 — Set-Piece Comeback Siege Multiplier

**Definition:** When a technically superior favorite trails a compact underdog and has credible wide delivery or set-piece specialists, team-corner and total-corner ceilings must be raised aggressively.

### Trigger conditions

Apply when at least 4 of 6 are true:

1. Favorite trails by 1 goal.
2. Underdog is likely to protect lead with compact/deep block.
3. Favorite has strong wide creators or set-piece takers.
4. Favorite needs points urgently in a tournament/bracket context.
5. Favorite has already produced 2+ corners or repeated blocked crosses before 60'.
6. Underdog's counterattacks are isolated rather than sustained.

### Adjustment

```text
Favorite_Team_Corners = Base_Team_Corners × 1.35 to 1.60
Total_Corners = Base_Total_Corners + 2.5 to 4.0
Over_8.5_Corners += 15 to 25 probability points
Over_9.5_Corners += 15 to 22 probability points
Unders at 8.5/9.5 should be downgraded to live-only or no-bet once trigger activates.
```

### Jordan-Algeria example

- Algeria trailed Jordan 1-0 from minute 36.
- Jordan's rational response was to defend deeper and protect the lead.
- Algeria had Riyad Mahrez as a high-quality set-piece and corner taker.
- Algeria needed the win to keep qualification alive.
- Algeria scored both comeback goals from corner/set-piece situations.

The correct live move after Jordan's goal was to kill or hedge Under 9.5 and upgrade Algeria team-corner overs.

## Pattern P12 — Corner Mechanism Beats Corner Average

**Definition:** Corner projections must be driven by attacking mechanism, not by generic possession, reputation, or average corners per match.

### Application

A favorite can dominate possession and still produce low corners when:

- It attacks centrally.
- It scores early and stops chasing.
- It creates clean shots without blocked crosses.
- The underdog presses high and prevents sustained box siege.

A team can generate high corners when:

- It is trailing.
- It attacks wide.
- Opponent clears repeatedly.
- Set-piece delivery is a major chance route.
- It cannot play clean central combinations and instead recycles wide pressure.

## Corner market tiering

### Tier 1 corner pick requires

- At least two current Grade A/B supports.
- Clear corner-generation mechanism.
- Scenario-tree survival in at least two branches.
- No obvious first-goal branch that destroys the pick.

### Tier 2 corner lean

Use when mechanism is clear but lineup/referee/market or recent team corner data is incomplete.

### Tier 3 live-only

Use when pre-match projection depends heavily on first goal or early attacking shape.

### No bet

Use when:

- Corner projection is based mainly on possession.
- Both teams attack centrally.
- First-goal state can flip the market.
- Team-specific corner data is missing.
- Line is already efficient.

## Live corner triggers

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Favorite trailing | Favorite down by 1 after 25' | Apply Rule #39 |
| Early favorite goal | Favorite leads before 30' | Reduce favorite corner ceiling unless GD chase remains |
| 0-0 at HT | Low shot volume and few crosses | Lean corner under unless both teams must win |
| Favorite has 3+ corners by 55' while trailing | Confirmed siege | Upgrade favorite team corners and total over |
| Underdog has no sustained counters | Pressure recycling | Raise favorite corners |
| Both teams wide and chasing | Open late game | Raise total corners |
| Red card to defending team | Team down to 10 and bunkering | Favorite corners up, goals not automatically up |
| Red card to attacking favorite | Favorite loses width/pressure | Corner ceiling down unless still attacking through set pieces |

---

## Betting discipline after Jordan-Algeria

1. Never label Under 9.5 as safe if the underdog-first-goal branch creates a favorite siege.
2. Prefer favorite team-corner overs over total corners when one team owns the mechanism.
3. If a set-piece specialist is central to the favorite's chance creation, upgrade corner quality and not just corner volume.
4. Corners and goals are linked only when set-piece conversion is strong; otherwise corners can rise without goals.
5. Live corner betting is often more reliable than pre-match corner betting because the first 15-25 minutes reveal the attacking mechanism.
