# Match Research Protocol — Anti-Blind-Spot Upgrade

Purpose: force every prediction to study the whole match, not just form tables, odds, or favorite/underdog reputation. This protocol adds coach-mindset analysis, tactical scenario trees, evidence grading, market survival checks, and blind-spot audits before any bet is recommended.

---

## 0. Core Principle

A football prediction is not a guess about which team is better. It is a structured forecast of how two coaching staffs are likely to solve a specific match problem under specific incentives, constraints, and game states.

Before giving probabilities, answer:

1. What does each coach want from this match?
2. What result is acceptable for each team?
3. What game state benefits each team?
4. What tactical plan gets each team there?
5. What could make the obvious read wrong?
6. Which claims are evidence-backed, and which are assumptions?

If these questions are not answered, confidence must be capped at 5/10.

---

## 1. Evidence Quality Ladder

Every major claim must be tagged with an evidence grade.

| Grade | Meaning | Examples | Model Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Direct, current, reliable | Official lineup, manager quote, official injury report, current match event data | Can move probability strongly |
| B | Current but indirect | Trusted beat report, recent tactical trend, bookmaker movement, recent xG data | Can move probability moderately |
| C | Historical but relevant | 2022+ H2H, coach tendency, tournament pattern | Small/moderate modifier only |
| D | Weak assumption | Reputation, old data, fan narrative, generic motivation | Mention only; do not anchor |
| X | Unknown/unverified | No reliable source found | Must be stated as uncertainty |

Rule: no Tier-1 betting pick may rely mainly on Grade C/D/X evidence. Tier-1 requires at least two independent Grade A/B supports.

## 2. Coach-Mindset Analysis

For each team, write a short coach-room diagnosis before probabilities.

### 2.1 Coach Objective

Identify the realistic objective:

- Win aggressively
- Win while controlling risk
- Accept a draw
- Avoid heavy defeat
- Protect goal difference
- Rotate and survive
- Chase margin for tiebreaker
- Force chaos because normal game favors opponent

### 2.2 Coach Fear

Identify what the coach is most afraid of:

- Early concession
- Transition exposure
- Set-piece weakness
- Losing midfield control
- Fullbacks being pinned
- Striker isolation
- Red card/foul trouble
- Fatigue collapse late
- Public pressure after poor previous match

### 2.3 Coach Tradeoff

Every plan has a cost. State it clearly.

Examples:

- High press creates chances but opens space behind.
- Deep block protects the box but invites shots and corners.
- Possession control reduces chaos but can become sterile.
- Direct countering creates threat but may surrender territory.
- Wide crossing creates corners but can be low-xG if the box is crowded.

If the analysis describes only advantages and no costs, it is incomplete.

---

## 3. Mentality Must Become Behavior

Do not use vague words like motivated unless the expected behavior is defined.

| Mentality Claim | Must Translate Into |
|---|---|
| Must win | Higher press, more attackers, earlier subs, riskier fullbacks |
| Draw is fine | Lower tempo, fewer bodies committed, time management |
| Underdog belief | Counter speed, set-piece aggression, emotional duel intensity |
| Favorite pressure | Rushed shots, individual actions, defensive impatience |
| Revenge/pride | Higher duel intensity, card risk, faster start |

A mentality claim that does not change expected behavior should not move the model.

---

## 4. Tactical Matchup Map

Before predicting markets, map the match in four phases.

### 4.1 Team A in Possession

- Build-up shape
- Main progression route: central, wide, long ball, half-space, set-piece
- Key creator
- Intended finisher
- Opponent's defensive answer
- Failure mode: sterile possession, turnovers, isolation, low-quality crosses

### 4.2 Team B in Possession

Use the same checks.

### 4.3 Team A Out of Possession

- Press height
- Trigger moments
- Defensive shape
- Weak zone
- Set-piece defense
- Transition defense

### 4.4 Team B Out of Possession

Use the same checks.

Rule: never say Team A will dominate possession as if that equals goal probability. Convert possession into shot quality, box entries, set pieces, and transition risk.

## 5. Scenario Tree — Whole-Match Forecasting

Every pre-match report must include at least four game-state branches.

### Scenario A — Favorite scores first early

Ask:

- Does the favorite keep pushing or manage the result?
- Does the underdog open up or stay damage-limitation?
- Do corners increase or collapse?
- Does Over 2.5 improve, or does the leader control tempo?

### Scenario B — Match is 0-0 at halftime

Ask:

- Who becomes nervous?
- Which coach has stronger bench options?
- Does draw become acceptable?
- Does second-half goal probability rise?

### Scenario C — Underdog scores first

Ask:

- Can the favorite break a deeper block?
- Does the underdog have enough defensive quality to absorb pressure?
- Does card/corner volume rise more than goal probability?
- Does the favorite become vulnerable to counters?

### Scenario D — Favorite leads by one after 70 minutes

Ask:

- Is there incentive to chase goal difference?
- Does the favorite have an elite late-surge pattern?
- Does the underdog still attack or protect respectability?
- Is live betting better than pre-match overs?

Markets must be checked against these scenarios. If a bet only wins in one scenario and loses in the other three, it is not a core pick.

---

## 6. Bracket, Table, and Incentive Logic

For tournaments, always document:

1. Current points, goal difference, goals scored/conceded.
2. Remaining fixtures for both teams.
3. Whether a draw is useful.
4. Whether goal difference matters.
5. Whether third-place qualification changes risk appetite.
6. Whether a team may rotate after taking a lead.
7. Whether the opponent's final group match changes today's urgency.

Do not write must win unless the table proves it. Do not write nothing to play for unless the table proves it.

---

## 7. History and H2H Rules

Historical data must be filtered.

Use:

- Matches from 2022 onward by default.
- Same coach/system era when possible.
- Similar opponent style, not just same opponent name.
- Similar competition pressure.
- Similar venue/weather/travel context if relevant.

Avoid:

- Old H2H from previous generations.
- National-team reputation without current squad evidence.
- Friendly results with rotated squads unless clearly relevant.
- One famous historical match as a psychological anchor.

History matters only when it explains a repeatable mechanism.

## 8. Blind-Spot Audit

Before finalizing, answer directly:

1. Am I overrating the favorite because of name/reputation?
2. Am I underrating the underdog's set pieces, counters, or goalkeeper performance?
3. Am I treating possession as goal threat without checking shot quality?
4. Am I assuming a team will chase goals when a draw or narrow loss may be acceptable?
5. Am I assuming motivation without translating it into tactics?
6. Am I using old data that no longer matches the current coach/squad?
7. Am I ignoring fatigue, travel, weather, pitch, or referee style?
8. Am I forcing a bet where the correct answer is no edge?
9. Am I confusing most dangerous player with most likely scorer?
10. Am I giving a correct-score pick too much confidence?

If two or more answers reveal major uncertainty, reduce confidence by at least 1 point.

---

## 9. Betting Pick Qualification

### Tier 1 — Core Pick

Requires:

- At least two independent Grade A/B supports.
- Wins in at least two major scenario branches.
- No major contradiction from tactical setup, bracket incentive, or market movement.
- Clear explanation of why the line is mispriced or safer than alternatives.

### Tier 2 — Lean

Use when:

- Logic is good but evidence is incomplete.
- Pick depends on lineup, early game state, or market price.
- Edge exists but variance is high.

### Tier 3 — Watch Live Only

Use when:

- Pre-match read depends heavily on first goal, tempo, or tactical reveal.
- Corners/cards/props lack enough evidence.
- Market can be read better after 10-20 minutes.

### No Bet

Use when:

- Market already reflects the model.
- Too many assumptions are unverified.
- Evidence conflicts.
- The match has high chaos and no stable edge.

No bet is a valid expert conclusion, not a failure.
